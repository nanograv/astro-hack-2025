NANOGrav AstroWG Hack Week 2025

Time: April 23rd — 25th (Wed—Fri)
Space: Berkeley, CA | Campbell Hall (Astronomy Dept.), UC Berkeley — 6th floor conference room



This hack-week will serve as a loosely-structured opportunity for collaborative work on AstroWG related projects, for example holodeck, and calculations for the upcoming PFC3 proposal. We envision that each day we will have some group discussion, a couple of brief presentations, and mostly open work time where people can self-organize into collaborative- or independent- work.

Please sign-up using the link above (includes 'maybe' option), which can also be used for coordinating shared hotel rooms. We are asking for a registration fee of $50/person for NANOGrav members, which is being supplemented by the NANOGrav PFC to cover coffee, snacks, lunches (Wed+Thur), and the group dinner (Thur). If costs of travel, or the registration fee, are an obstacle to any attendees, please reach out to Luke Kelley as there may be some limited funding available to assist.

Travel

Flights: the UC Berkeley campus is located right next to downtown Berkeley. Getting to Berkeley is very easy from either San Francisco International Airport (SFO; more/cheaper flights) or Oakland International Airport (OAK; a little faster/easier). San Jose International Airport (SJC) is also not bad, but requires a few transfers.

Local: campus, and the local area, are both very easily accessible via both BART light-rail and AC Transit Bus. You can pay for both using a Clipper Card or Clipper phone app.

Hotels: the best local hotel for visiting campus is the Residence Inn Downtown Berkeley, which is right next to campus and also the Downtown Berkeley BART station. The Hotel Shattuck Plaza is just around the corner (but I can't personally vouch for it). Some cheaper, still good options are the Graduate (Hilton) (also right next to campus), and the Aiden (Best Western) which is an easy ~10 minute bus ride away.
NOTE: There are a number of other options on University Avenue, running west from campus; unfortunately, many of them are a bit run-down, and may be in uncomfortable areas.
